

Here is a drafted User Journey based on the real-world feedback from the Facebook groups. This narrative contrasts the "Old Way" (chaos, fear, screenshots) with the "Rental Trust Way" (security, verified facts, control).

### The User Journey: "The Safe Rental"

**Personas:**
*   **Alex (The Tenant):** Anxious about identity theft, tired of emailing IDs, has a good rental history but hates sharing sensitive data.
*   **Sam (The Landlord):** Skeptical of fake reference letters, tired of "gut feeling" failures, wants proof of income but respects privacy.

---

### Phase 1: The Inquiry (Before Rental Trust)

**The Old Way (Current Reality):**
*   **Sam** posts an ad on Facebook Marketplace.
*   **Alex** sees the ad and messages Sam.
*   **Sam (Automated Reply):** *"Please email me a copy of your Passport, Driver’s License, and latest Paystub to book a viewing."*
*   **Alex’s Feeling:** Panic. *"Is this a scam? If I email this, will he sell my ID? What if I get there and the place is a dump? He has my data forever."*
*   **Outcome:** Alex ghosts the ad to stay safe. Sam loses a good tenant.

---

### Phase 2: The Setup (Rental Trust Implementation)

**1. Alex (Tenant) Sets Up the "Vault":**
*   Alex downloads **Rental Trust**.
*   Alex scans Passport & ID once.
*   Alex uploads Credit Report.
*   **System Action:** The platform verifies the ID (checking liveness and document authenticity).
*   **Constitution Rule:** *Data Ownership.* These documents live in Alex's encrypted vault, not on a landlord's server.

**2. Sam (Landlord) Sets Up the "Criteria":**
*   Sam signs up for **Rental Trust**.
*   Sam verifies his identity (Property Title check).
*   Sam sets his viewing requirements: *"Must show Verified Identity + Income > 3x Rent."*

---

### Phase 3: The Viewing (The "Temporary Handshake")

**The New Way (With Rental Trust):**
*   **Alex** messages Sam: *"I’m interested in the unit. Here is my Rental Trust Profile link."*
*   **Sam** clicks the link. It opens a secure window.
*   **The "Share" Event:** Alex's app asks: *"Sam wants to view your Identity Profile. Grant Access?"*
*   **Alex selects:** *"Grant Access for 24 hours (Viewing Only)."*
*   **Constitution Rule:** *Sharing ≠ Copying.* Sam can view the verified documents on the screen, but **cannot download, print, or email them.**

**The Scenario Plays Out:**
*   Sam sees "✅ Identity Verified" and "✅ Income Verified." He approves the viewing.
*   Alex goes to the apartment. It’s dirty and smells like smoke.
*   **The Twist:** Alex leaves the building. While walking away, he opens the app and clicks **"Revoke Access."**
*   **Sam's Reality:** The link he was looking at instantly dies. He no longer has access to Alex's ID. Alex feels safe.

---

### Phase 4: The Application (The "Verified Facts")

**The Old Way (Current Reality):**
*   **Sam** asks for "3 months bank statements."
*   **Alex** screenshots his e-transfers (blurring out balance) or sends a PDF of his statement.
*   **Sam** thinks: *"Are these photoshopped? Did he just edit the numbers? Did he borrow money from a friend to make the balance look good?"*

**The New Way (With Rental Trust):**
*   Alex finds a unit he loves (Landlord: Sarah).
*   Alex grants **"Full Application Access"** to Sarah via Rental Trust.
*   **The "Facts Only" View:** Sarah sees:
    *   **Identity:** Verified (Green Checkmark).
    *   **Rental History:** *"Previous Address: 123 Main St. Rent: $1,800. Status: Paid On-Time for 12/12 months (Verified by Bank Link)."*
    *   **Income:** *"Verified by Employer Payroll API."*
*   **Sarah's Feeling:** Confidence. *"This isn't a screenshot he made. This is a fact verified by the bank."*
*   **Constitution Rule:** *Facts Only.* No judgment, no "risk score." Just raw data.

---

### Phase 5: The Lease (The "Static Record")

**The Old Way:**
*   Sarah emails a PDF lease.
*   Alex signs, scans, and emails it back.
*   Sarah keeps the PDF in a folder called "Leases" on her Google Drive.
*   **Risk:** If Alex revokes his email account later or if Sarah gets hacked, the lease is messy.

**The New Way (With Rental Trust):**
*   Sarah generates a **Province-Compliant Lease** inside the app.
*   Both parties digitally sign.
*   **The "Legal Exception":** The system instantly generates a watermarked **"Static Legal Record"** (PDF).
*   **Alex's View:** Gets a copy in his vault.
*   **Sarah's View:** Gets a copy in her vault.
*   **Constitution Rule:** *Legal Retention Exception.* Even if Alex deletes his account later, Sarah keeps the "Static Legal Record" for her legal files. The lease is safe.

---

### Phase 6: The Tenancy (Building the "Trust Ledger")

*   Rent is due on the 1st.
*   Alex pays via Interac e-Transfer directly to Sarah.
*   Sarah uploads a "Receipt" or the app connects to the bank to confirm receipt.
*   **Platform Action:** Rental Trust appends a record: *"Oct 1: Rent Paid - On Time."*
*   **Future Value:** When Alex moves out in 2 years, he has a 2-year unbroken chain of "Paid On Time" records.
*   **Next Landlord:** Alex doesn't need to beg for a reference. He just shows his **Rental Trust Ledger**.

---

### Summary: Why This Wins

| Feedback Point | Rental Trust Solution |
| :--- | :--- |
| *"I hate emailing IDs to strangers"* | **View-Only Access:** Landlord sees it, can't keep it. Access is revoked instantly. |
| *"Screenshots are easy to fake"* | **Verified Ledger:** History comes from bank/payroll APIs, not from the tenant's phone. |
| *"Reference letters are useless"* | **Historical Fact Record:** A timeline of actual payments, replacing the "he said/she said" of phone calls. |
| *"I need to keep the lease for tax reasons"* | **Static Legal Record:** Separate from the revocable vault, ensuring landlord compliance. |
