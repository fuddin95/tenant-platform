Document 2: Business Plan Context & Strategy
Rental Trust — Strategic Business Context

1. Executive Summary
Rental Trust is a neutral "Trust Infrastructure" layer for the Canadian rental market. Unlike marketplaces (e.g., Zillow, Realtor.ca) that facilitate discovery, Rental Trust facilitates the execution and verification of the rental lifecycle. By creating a portable, tenant-owned ledger of rental history, we solve the twin problems of rental fraud (for tenants) and unverifiable risk (for landlords).

2. Market Problem & Opportunity
The Scam Epidemic: In Toronto and Vancouver, rental scams involving fake listings and identity theft are rampant. Tenants have no way to verify if a landlord actually owns the property.
The Data Void: Canada lacks a portable rental credit standard. Good tenants with no "official" credit history are treated as risky; bad tenants simply hop between landlords who don't talk to each other.
The "Paper" Gap: Residential Tenancy Boards (LTB/RTB) are clogged because contracts are often vague or non-existent, and evidence is lost.
3. Solution: The "Trust Protocol"
We replace "trust me" with "verify this."

For Tenants: A "Digital Rental Wallet" where they own their identity, documents, and history. They can grant access to a landlord instantly without sending 50 emails.
For Landlords: A "Risk Dashboard" based on verified facts (not scores) and a legally compliant lease generation engine.
4. Product Strategy (The Wedge and The Scale)
Phase 1: The "Anti-Scam" Wedge (MVP)

Core Feature: Landlord Identity Verification.
Landlords pay a small fee or undergo a rigorous check to prove they own the property (Title Deed matching).
Value Prop: "This listing is Verified by Rental Trust."
Core Feature: Tenant Secure Vault.
Tenants upload ID/Credit once.
Revenue: Freemium for Tenants (Free storage), Fee for Landlords (Verification badge).
Phase 2: The "Digital Handshake" (Adoption)

Core Feature: Province-Compliant Digital Leases.
We generate leases specific to Ontario/BC regulations that are legally binding.
Signed leases are hashed and stored on the blockchain (or equivalent append-only ledger).
Value Prop: "Sign your lease in 5 minutes, fully compliant."
Revenue: SaaS subscription per unit for Property Managers; Transaction fee for independent landlords.
Phase 3: The "Portable Reputation" (The Moat)

Core Feature: Automated Rental History.
Because the lease was signed on Rental Trust, we can now record payment events (if integrated with rent payment) or manual "attestations" of on-time rent.
Tenants take this "Verified History" to their next rental application.
Revenue: API access fees for large property management companies to pull verified tenant histories.
5. Monetization Philosophy
We monetize the workflow, not the data.

Identity Verification Services: Charging landlords to verify they are not scammers.
Workflow Efficiency Tools: Charging for the digital lease signing and maintenance request tools (B2B SaaS).
Verified History Access: Charging future landlords a small fee to access a tenant's "Verified Record" (similar to a credit check fee, but the money is split with the tenant as a data dividend).
6. Operational Risks & Mitigations
Risk A: The "Lazy Landlord" Resistance

Problem: Landlords don't want to learn a new system; they want a simple "Yes/No."
Mitigation: Our UI presents "Facts" as a visual confidence meter (Green/Yellow) based on factual completeness, not a personality score. We give them the simplicity of a score without the liability.
Risk B: The "Cold Start" Problem

Problem: The platform is useless without history, but we have no history to start with.
Mitigation: We focus on Phase 2 (Lease Signing). The history builds organically. We don't need to import old data; we just need to capture the new data correctly from Day 1.
Risk C: Legal Compliance (Data Retention)

Problem: Landlords must keep leases; Tenants want to revoke access.
Mitigation: The "Static Legal Record" feature in the Constitution. We create a separate, non-revocable file for the landlord that satisfies the law, while keeping the tenant's live profile under tenant control.
7. Long-Term Vision
To become the "Operating System" for Canadian Renting.

Tenants arrive in Canada, open a Rental Trust account, and build a reputation from their first basement apartment to their luxury condo.
Landlords stop asking for "Credit Scores" (which are archaic) and start asking for "Rental Trust Verified History."
